Varieties

RKATSITELI

This white grape variety belongs to the group of Kakheti vine varieties and is most common in Georgia. In addition, it is widespread in other regions. Rkatsiteli grape juice maintains the desired ratio of sugar-acidity until the beginning of physiological ripeness. Reaches full maturity from mid-September to early October. The best microzones for Rkatsiteli are: Kardenakhi, Tibaani, Tsinandali, Gurjaani, Napareuli. High quality high quality classical and Kakhetian traditional wines are made from Rkatsiteli variety in Georgia, often used together with green Kakhetian.

TCOLIKAURI

Georgian white grape vine belongs to the Imereti aboriginal vine group. In terms of area, it ranks second after Rkatsiteli in Georgia. Apart from Imereti, it is widespread in Racha-Lechkhumi, Guria, Samegrelo, Adjara and Abkhazia. It is from the late period and it produces quite full-bodied wine with tones of citrus and flowers. It can be said that Tsolikouri is a valuable vine variety, it is moderately adapted to environmental conditions, abundant yield, high quality of products and relatively good resistance to diseases.
